Overview


This multimedia CD explains the origin and characteristics of heart sounds and murmurs and provides auditory and visual waveform examples of the most common of these sounds. Visual displays of time amplitude plots of the various sounds are provided in time expansion mode to aid in discerning the details of the sound. Illustrations of the mechanisms of production of heart sounds are also shown in a Murmur Mechanism Guide to facilitate learning.

A special feature for the student is the ability to get definitions of terms that may be unfamiliar by pointing the cursor at the highlighted text or by clicking on it.

 

How to listen to the sounds

In order to use this software, you are required to have a Macromedia Flash Player installed on the computer. Please refer to the "System Requirements" sections for instructions on how to install the Flash Player. The sounds on this CD are of high quality and closely resemble real sounds heard though the stethoscope. Heart sounds are low frequency sounds. We strongly recommend that headphones be used for listening to this CD. To make sure that your headphones are capable of reproducing the heart sounds we are providing the following test. Please playback the following three tones.

Example of 600Hz
Example of 150Hz
Example of 35Hz

If you were unable to hear any of the 3 tones you are NOT likely to hear an accurate reproduction of the heart and lung sounds.

Sound waveform is shown unexpanded (top) and expanded (bottom). An unexpanded waveform shows four or more heart cycles. One of the cycles is highlighted in orange, and is shown on the bottom in an expanded form.

Audience

Intended for everyone interested in clinical medicine, particularly students, interns, residents, practitioners, nurses, physician assistants, and intensivists. May also be helpful as a referral base of data for the advanced cardiovascular trainee.
