Ventricular Septal Defect

Tricuspid Area Murmur
[4th Intercostal space on the left]
Systolic

 

  • Pitch and intensity may vary depending on the size of the defect and the pressure differential across the septum.
  • Usually holosystolic, may be ejection type in character.
  • May be associated with other murmurs, i.e.: mitral regurgitation, aortic insufficiency etc. depending upon other co-dominant abnormalities.
  • Usually congenital in origin, may be acquired in the face of myocardial infarction (usually inferior but may be seen anteriorly as well).
  • Murmur may disappear if Eisenmenger’s physiology becomes present.
